from, EXCERPTS_P2_Que-hace-la-SCJN.txt

writ
a legal document from a law court that tells you that you will be involved in a legal process and explains what you must do

Highlights--
*	The "writ of support" can be used to challenge laws, regulations, or actions by government officials that are deemed unconstitutional or that violate individual rights.

*	Mexico is one of many nations that support human rights in this manner

*	Applies to any and all person that find themselves in Mexico including minors, private companies, officials, and foreigners

*	 6 Jun 2011 Mexico updated and extended its support for human rights to align with international 
	treaties
		- writ of support available for both Constitutional violation and treaty violations
		- writ of support available as a class action suit
		- writ of support available not only in cases of legal interest but also for cases of 
		legitimate interest
		- writ of support available for plantiffs and defendents
		- The SCJN has formed a lower court to handle human rights cases
		- The SCJN has established new sanctions for non-compliance
		- The SCJN has eliminated the statute of limitations for cases
		- The SCJN will not close a case until all sentencing has been completed
		- The SCJN has granted the Executive Branch rapid access to some (urgent) cases
		- The SCJN has limited the abuse of a suspension clause that would permit the continued 
		infringement or violation 
		  of rights of the affected person
